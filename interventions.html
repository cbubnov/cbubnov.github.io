Interventions is a one-day conference scheduled to take place on April 7th 2018. It will be comprised of speakers, panels, and workshops, and discusses the role of creative thinkers in developing brands, products and services in an age of rapid technological advancement. It is run by Scout, Northeastern University's student-led design studio, and backed by the support of the College of Arts, Media, and Design. We have attracted the attention of several companies and there are scheduled appearances from companies such as Dribbble, Airbnb, and Netflix.





EXPLORATIONS





We explored a lot of visual directions and researched potential speakers that were relevant to the theme of Interventions: how does design intervene in the world, for better or worse? We coordinated with the logistics team, who acted as the "clients" of the semester-long project. This type of project was a first for Scout and all members involved, so the objective was vague going into it. We spent a lot of time brainstorming ideas, names, concepts, and visual directions. We put up a website and planned for its different states - before, during, and after the conference. We created wayfinding examples and mock schedules.





BRAND





Poppins was chosen for its delicate balance of fun and professionalism. Paired with Karla's robotic-infused warmth, the typographic duo works in tandem to convey a personable yet future-focused product. Setting Karla in all caps vs regular for different purposes allowed each to come to life in its own way.

I chose a simple color palette that tweaked the original blue and orange colors of the site to have more contrast while also being more gentle on the eye. I combined these with lots of grey shades. This was to keep the audience's higher percentage of color blindness in mind.
