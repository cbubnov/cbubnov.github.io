Interventions is a one-day conference scheduled to take place on April 7th 2018. It will be comprised of speakers, panels, and workshops, and discusses the role of creative thinkers in developing brands, products and services in an age of rapid technological advancement. It is run by Scout, Northeastern University's student-led design studio, and backed by the support of the College of Arts, Media, and Design. There are scheduled appearances from companies such as Dribbble, Airbnb, and Netflix.





EXPLORATIONS





We explored a lot of visual directions and researched potential speakers that were relevant to the theme of Interventions: how does design intervene in the world, for better or worse? We coordinated with the logistics team, who acted as the "clients" of the semester-long project. This type of project was a first for Scout and all members involved, so the objective was vague going into it. We spent a lot of time brainstorming ideas, names, concepts, and visual directions. We put up a website and planned for its different states - before, during, and after the conference. We created wayfinding examples and mock schedules.





BRAND





Our primary typefaces are Rubik and Sentinel Italic. Sentinel should only be used in all lower-case italic, otherwise it looks too old-fashioned. These typefaces shall be used consistently in order to unify the appearance of all visual materials.

This color palette was chosen to exude a fun, dynamic atmosphere - the conference is a place with an energetic, purposeful vibe that is contagious. The colors work in both RGB and CMYK colorspaces, to maintain consistency between print, web, and social collateral.
